Introduction

The methodology of Creative Problem Solving has two main foundations: affirmative thinking and suspension of judgment. Both are backed by multiple studies in the area of neuroscience.

Affirmative Thought

It makes the limbic system of the brain activate the neocortical system responsible for cognitive processes. It is the part of the brain we use to think, create, devise, solve.

Suspension of Judgement

Refers to the separation of two mental processes: creation of options and its evaluation. It doesn't mean we don't have to evaluate and judge, it's about learning to choose the right time for it.

Process

The methodology covers the whole cycle of problem solving: from analyzing the situation and the formulation of the challenge to the development of the innovative and original idea into a plan of action to carry out the solution.
