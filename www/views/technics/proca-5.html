Step Four
A-B-C Method

Now let's sort the list of tasks and subtasks by importance. You may assign an A, B or C to each task. Ask yourself: what would happen if I leave this for tomorrow?

A:Serious consequences. You will only assign this letter to the most important and essential tasks. It's something you must do no matter what.

B:Light consequences. These are tasks that are also important, but whose consequences are lighter than those of letter A if postponed.

C:No consequences. These are taks of little relevance. Nothing would happen if you postponed them for tomorrow.

Use this method to realize how much you procrastinate. When there are A tasks to be done and you've spent time on B and C tasks: you've procrastinated. If you postpone tasks due to lack of time, make sure they're C or B tasks, but make sure to make all A.
